CABA Putah Creek Facility

The Center for Aquatic Biology and Aquaculture (CABA) was established to provide leadership, focus, and support to University of California Davis researchers in addressing problems associated with California’s cultured and wild aquatic biological resources.

The Putah Creek facility consists of two buildings for inside work (approx. 1800 sq. ft.) with an office trailer and tool room. This facility has mainly large diameter tank systems (7’ to 20’ dia.) suitable for large species of fish or for use in mesocosm studies.
